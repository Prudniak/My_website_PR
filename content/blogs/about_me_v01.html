Life in Warsaw

I was born and raised in the capital of Poland, Warsaw. Living in this great and vibrant city for so many years was an amazing experience, and I invite everyone to come to see it for yourself!

Bachelor’s degree at Warsaw School of Economics

I enjoyed going to the primary, secondary and high school, as I met a lot of interesting people that got me inspired on everyday basis. Eventually, I decided to stay in Warsaw to pursue Bachelor’s diploma in Quantitative Methods in Economics and Information Systems at Warsaw School of Economics.

(Picture of me sailing in the Mazurian Lakes region in Poland)

Moreover, the school gave me an opportunity to participate in foreign exchange programme at University of St. Gallen, where I have spent one incredible semester!

Professional life and the future…

After my first year of studies, I got into PwC as part of the WEX program, where I spent a month in the business consulting department, where I had my first contact with consultant work, in which I liked the way of approaching difficult problems.

And it all made me decide to get into the research department at McKinsey, where I have supported 30+ different projects and developed insights into the energy and TMT sectors in Poland. This has convinced me to do an internship as a junior business analyst at Kearney, during which I have worked on a cost optimization project for a leading Romanian telco.

In future I would see myself working as a data-analytics consultant in a strategic advisory company, however, I am open to all opportunities…
